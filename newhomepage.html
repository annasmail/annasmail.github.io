My background...

Art or Science?
My first experience of graphic design came in Year 7 where we had to make a menu for a fish and chips restaurant and I instantly loved it! In the following years, I continued to enjoy art and design classes, but tended to prioritise science and maths.
When it came to picking a degree, I decided on ‘product design engineering’ as the perfect middle ground between science and art. I started my course in 2020 and got some fun experience with working to briefs and learning new software like Affinity and Fusion360.
Finding the middle ground
After a year of designing dental-floss dispensers and garden equipment, I realised that by finding the middle ground, I had ended up doing neither of my interests. I decided to change course - to physics and earth sciences.
I loved the course and was pleased with the change, but I still gravitated back to design: leaning more into the data presentation and mapping aspects of my degree, as well as developing my illustration skills for personal projects and university societies.
Discovering web design!
I started a Masters in Quantitative Climate and Environmental Science, where I discovered my love for coding! At the same time, the Cambridge Climate Society were looking for a new designer for their website.
Now combining my three interests: climate science, coding AND design, I spent a bit too much time learning web design. I used mainly used WebFlow, but also created my own Github webpage, learning HTML, CSS and JavaScript.
And now...
I have just graduated and am now in training to become an operational meteorologist. With a shift-style work schedule, I have quite a bit of free time and am hoping to continue to develop my skills in web design, data presentation and scientific communication! This webpage is the current work in progress :)
