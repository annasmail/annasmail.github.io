The use of magnets in medicine started around 70 years ago. In 1957, Gilchrist injected tumours with biocompatible, magnetic nanoparticles (MNPs) and then used the particlesâ€™ magnetic property to heat them up. This successfully destroyed the metastases while not affecting the surrounding healthy tissue. Current research focuses on the use of external magnets to guide and trap drug-carrying MNPs. This has the promising ability to concentrate drugs at disease locations by pulling the MNPs towards the external magnet thereby minimising exposure of healthy tissues to potentially cytotoxic drugs.
