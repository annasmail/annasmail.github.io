Atmospheric Temperature

Mathematically describing the observed temperature patterns of Earth's atmosphere.
next

On the left is a plot of the Earth's atmospheric temperature. Two temperature inversions are highlighted, defining the atmospheric layers: stratosphere and thermosphere. But why does this pattern occur? Furthermore, why does temperature decrease in the troposphere and mesosphere? This post will outline the physical causes for these observations.

The Barometric Law

First, we need to derive the barometric law which can describe how certain physical attributes such as pressure and density change with altitude, z. For this, we define the rate of change of pressure as a linear decrease, proportional to gravity. We will also use the ideal gas equation.

$$ {\frac{dp}{dz} = -gp}\qquad{p = \frac{\rho k_B T}{\mu m_p}}\\$$

By assuming T, u and g as constant with respect to altitude, we can sub the ideal gas equation into -gp and then integrate with respect to z giving

$$ {p(z) = p(0)exp(-\frac{z}{H}}\qquad{H = \frac{k_B T}{g \mu m_p}\\$$ where H is defined as the atmospheric scale height (8.5km for Earth). The above equation shows the exponential decrease in pressure with z, but as pressure is proportional to density (ref. ideal gas equation) we can also define the following equation. $$ {\rho(z) = \rho(0)exp(-\frac{z}{H}}\qquad{H = \frac{k_B T}{g \mu m_p}\\$$
