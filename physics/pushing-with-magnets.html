Pushing

with Magnets

How can magnets be used to ‘push’ magnetic nanoparticles?
What is the optimal arrangement of two magnets for drug delivery?

A variety of magnetic arrangements have successfully guided and concentrated drug-carrying magnetic nanoparticles to disease locations. This is typically done by first dispersing drugs around the body, then applying an attractive magnetic field to pull the drugs towards the diseased location.

Due to the rapid decay of magnetic fields over distance, magnetic drug targeting is limited to anatomical structures close to the skins’ surface.

Components of the inner ear such as the cochlea and semicircular canals are responsible for auditory processing and balance and are affected by conditions such as tinnitus.

Intratympanic injection is a common drug delivery method but in order to reach the cochlea, drugs must diffuse through the semi-permeable round window membrane (RWM) meaning only 1 in 107 molecules actually reach the cochlea.

If magnets can push MNPs, they could help drive drugs through the RWM.
